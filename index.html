Cybersecurity Concepts

Important cybersecurity concepts that you NEED to know.

The CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is a model that represents three critical aspects of cybersecurity that must be considered and implemented into any system.

https://www.f5.com/labs/articles/what-is-the-cia-triad

Confidentiality

Keeping sensitive data private and preventing unauthorized access.

Integrity

Ensuring data is accurate and protected from accidental or malicious modification.

Availability

Making sure that data and systems are accessible to authorized users when needed.

The States of Data

Data exists in three distincts states, which each have different properties and are found in different areas.

Data at Rest

Storage - Data stored on hard drives, USB drives, memory, DVDs, ect.

Data in Transit

Transmission - Data moving from one system to another via LAN, Internet, ect.

Data in Use

Processing - Data being created, used in application, being processed, placed in memory, ect.

Methods of Authentication

Authentication methods are used to verify a user's identity to ensure that only authorized people can access whatever is being secured. There are three main types of authentication, being something you have, something you know, and something you are.

https://www.descope.com/learn/post/adaptive-authentication

Password

Something you know

Uses a secret password or pin to authenticate.

Card

Something you have

Cards embedded with a chip that is automatically recognized by an authenticating device.

Certificate

Something you have

Servers use certificates to authenticate remotely. To use a certificate, server owners must provide a third party authority with documentation.

Biometrics

Something you are

Biometrics use a measurable physical characteristic to verify identity such as fingerprint, retina scan, voice, ect.

Multi-Factor Authentication

Something you have and know

Uses more than one method of authentication to increase security. An example is Google's two-step verification.

Passwords

Passwords are extremely important for securing online accounts and data, which is why strong passwords are so important. Because of this, there are a few rules that should be followed when creating a strong password.

-----

-For a strong password-

Use 8-10 characters MINIMUM.

Use a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, along with special characters and symbols.

Do NOT use any personal information, such as family names, birthdates, pet names, ect.

Do NOT reuse passwords on important websites.

Do NOT use common dictionary words.

Phishing

Phishing is the use of bogus emails and websites to trick you into supplying confidential or personal information. It is used in the majority of cyber attacks and should always be removed and reported.

-----

-To spot a phishing email-

Look for misspellings or incorrect grammar.

Check if the sender and their email address don't seem to match.

Hover over a link they sent, and check if the URL doesn't match the text.

Social Engineering

Social engineering is a manipulation technique that exploits human psychology and trust to get people to reveal confidential information or grant access to unauthorized systems or even physical locations. It is important to know common social engineering techniques to be able to recognize and avoid them.

https://us.norton.com/blog/emerging-threats/what-is-social-engineering

Baiting

Using the promise of a free item or other thing of value.

Shoulder Surfing

Looking over someone's shoulder to steal valuable information or passwords.

Dumpster Diving

Searching through trash to find information that can be used for cyber attacks.

Info Written in Workspace

Looking at passwords or codes written around the workspace on paper or post-its.

Piggybacking

Following someone to get into a restricted space.

Pretexting

Wearing a uniform or using authority to access something restricted.

Using impersonation while rushing or having an emergency to access something restricted.

Scareware and Ransomware

Using fake or real messages to force users to take an action like installing software or paying and ransom.

Potential Threats

Many potential threats may threaten a person, system, or data, and being able to recognize some of those threats is crucial to spotting, preventing, and fixing them if they arise.

Hackers

Hackers may attempt to access accounts, data, or systems in order to gain something.

Malware

Malware may attempt to infiltrate a system in order to steal information and data or to block the user from the system.

Data Breaches

Hackers may sometimes get into large data bases and release sensitive information that could compromise accounts, data, and personal imformation.
